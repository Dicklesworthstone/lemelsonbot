# Anti-Pattern Library — Lemelson Invention Method

## A1 — Undated or Unwitnessed Notes
**What not to do**: Record ideas without dates or witness signatures.
**Why it fails**: Weakens priority claims and credibility.
**Anchors**: §2, §11, §12

## A2 — Disclosure Without Logging or Terms
**What not to do**: Share concepts or models without a disclosure log or confidentiality terms.
**Why it fails**: Ideas can leak without a defensible record.
**Anchors**: §13, §16

## A3 — No Manufacturing Method
**What not to do**: Describe a concept without specifying materials/processes.
**Why it fails**: Results in unbuildable or ambiguous claims.
**Anchors**: §20, §22

## A4 — Single-Embodiment Fixation
**What not to do**: Keep only one embodiment or example.
**Why it fails**: Narrows scope and leaves claim space unused.
**Anchors**: §7, §14

## A5 — Experiments Without Records
**What not to do**: Run material/process tests without logging procedures/results.
**Why it fails**: Loses learning and weakens evidentiary trail.
**Anchors**: §5, §8

## A6 — Prototype Without Context
**What not to do**: Show a model without recording who saw it or what was learned.
**Why it fails**: Breaks the chain from prototype to claim and partner feedback.
**Anchors**: §6, §17
